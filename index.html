Our Story

Where the Apennines
meet London Fields

Auguste is built around the pastoral cooking of central Italy—the rugged, elemental food of the Abruzzo highlands, carried faithfully across the Apennines and onto a single grill in East London.

At the heart of the kitchen: arrosticini—flame-grilled skewers of hand-cut meat cooked over charcoal, eaten standing, eaten slowly, eaten with wine. No fuss. No theatre.

The space is deliberately imperfect: mismatched chairs, low candlelight, the faint blue haze of a room that has housed a thousand conversations. Come as you are. Stay as long as you like.
